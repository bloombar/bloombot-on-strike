# AFFIDAVIT OUTLINE

## Purpose

Affidavits from friends, family members or other third parties, stating in great detail, just as though you were testifying in court, what it is about the MARRIED COUPLE that leads you to believe that this is a legitimate marriage entered into for love and not just to help the applicant get a green card.

## Required Information which Should be Included

All affidavits must contain full name, date and place of birth and address of the person making the affidavit, your relationship to the applicant or the spouse, and must be signed by the you and notarized by a notary public;

## Helpful Information which Should be Included

- The length of time you have known the applicant and spouse;
- Describe your knowledge about the applicant’s marital relationship, courtship and/or marriage ceremony;
- Describe your knowledge of the fact that the applicant and spouse resided and continue to reside together;
- Describe any occasions where you had the chance to observe the couple, i.e. social gatherings; their home; etc.;
- Describe any visible signs of a marital union between the applicant or his/her spouse that you observed;
- Describe any instances when you were told by the applicant or the spouse their marital relationship;

# Letter

Affidavit of Alexandra Franskevich

I, Amos Bloomberg, being duly sworn, state the following:

1. My full name is Amos Bloomberg. I was born on 10/3/1975 in Watford, England. I currently reside at 13 Cleveland Drive, Croton-on-Hudson, NY 10520.

2. I make this affidavit based on my personal knowledge and observations regarding the marriage of Alexandra Franskevich and Yitz [Last Name].

3. I have known Alexandra and Yitz for approximately four (4) years.

4. During the first approximately two (2) years that I knew them, I observed what I believed to be an authentic and loving marriage. They presented themselves as a genuine couple, spent meaningful time together, and interacted in a way consistent with a real marital relationship.

5. Over the course of my interactions with them, I had opportunities to observe them at social occasions and in private settings. Based on those observations, I believed their marriage was entered into in good faith and for genuine personal reasons.

6. Around two (2) years ago, I began to observe and learn of a serious decline in the marriage. The relationship changed significantly and, to my understanding, the marriage began to break down due to irreconcilable differences.

7. Since approximately that time, I observed behavior by Yitz that I understood to be manipulative and, from Alexandra's perspective and as communicated to me, unforgivable. These developments were a substantial factor in the deterioration of the marriage.

8. Based on my personal knowledge, the marriage between Alexandra and Yitz was real and bona fide, but it unfortunately later failed due to these irreconcilable differences and harmful conduct.

I declare under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and correct to the best of my knowledge.
